<p><p>The supergrains market encompasses various ancient grains known for their nutritional benefits and versatility. Freekeh is a roasted green wheat, prized for its nutty flavor and high fiber content. Kamut is an ancient wheat variety, rich in protein and minerals. Teff, a tiny Ethiopian grain, is gluten-free and high in iron. Millet is a drought-resistant grain, suitable for diverse diets. Bulgur, cracked wheat, offers quick cooking times and nutty taste. Buckwheat is a gluten-free seed rich in antioxidants, while fonio is a fast-growing, nutritious grain popular in West Africa. Collectively, these supergrains cater to health-conscious consumers seeking alternative sources of nutrition.</p></p>
