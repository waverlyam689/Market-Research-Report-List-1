<p><p>The medical grade microcrystalline cellulose market is categorized into wood pulp-based and refined cotton-based types. Wood pulp-based microcrystalline cellulose is derived from natural cellulose sources and is often favored for its cost-effectiveness and structural properties. Refined cotton-based microcrystalline cellulose, on the other hand, is known for its purity and is typically used in high-end pharmaceutical formulations. Both types serve as excipients in drug formulations, providing stability, texture, and improved bioavailability in various medical applications.</p></p>
