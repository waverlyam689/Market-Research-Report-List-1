<p><p>The clotting factor market is segmented into several types based on specific factors involved in blood coagulation. Clotting Factor I refers to fibrinogen, essential for forming blood clots. Clotting Factor II, or prothrombin, plays a critical role in the clotting cascade. Clotting Factor III, known as tissue factor, initiates coagulation. Clotting Factor IV represents calcium ions, vital for multiple coagulation processes. Other factors encompass additional proteins and enzymes involved in clot formation, crucial for managing bleeding disorders and enhancing surgical outcomes.</p></p>
