<p><p>The lithography market utilizes various light sources for semiconductor manufacturing. Deep Ultraviolet (DUV) light sources, including Argon Fluoride (ArF), Krypton Fluoride (KrF), and i-line, are essential for patterning integrated circuits at smaller nodes. ArF offers shorter wavelengths for advanced nodes, while KrF serves in older nodes. The Extreme Ultraviolet (EUV) light source represents a cutting-edge technology with even shorter wavelengths, enabling the production of complex, high-density patterns essential for the latest generation of microchips. Both types are critical for advancing semiconductor capabilities.</p></p>
